Our Mission

Dedicated care for first responders

PFC Associates operates the Police and Fire Clinic (PFC) in Washington, DC — a specialized occupational health facility providing comprehensive medical services to Metropolitan Police, Fire and EMS, US Secret Service, and US Park Police personnel.

Our integrated approach combines primary care, behavioral health, physical rehabilitation, and diagnostic services under one roof, ensuring first responders receive the focused, timely care they deserve.

What We Offer

Integrated health services

From routine care to specialized treatment, every service is designed around the unique demands of public safety work.

Common Questions

Frequently asked

The Police and Fire Clinic is open 0700–2300 hours (7 AM – 11 PM), Monday through Friday. The clinic is closed during major holidays.

After hours, members can visit MedStar Washington Hospital Center, MedStar Georgetown University Hospital Center Emergency Rooms, MedStar Prompt Care (Adams Morgan or Capitol Hill), or Providence Urgent Care Center depending on severity.

PFC does not cover outside specialists unless referred by the clinic. Your private insurance may deny reimbursement since treatment involves workers' compensation.

Wear loose-fitting clothing that allows access to the injured area — sweats or shorts for lower body injuries, sleeveless shirts for shoulder issues. Bring supportive shoes; no flip-flops.

An MRI uses a powerful magnet — wear regular clothing without jewelry or hairpins. Officers must secure weapons beforehand. Patients requiring sedation will need to arrange transportation.

Complete a medical records release form with pertinent details and submit via mail, fax, or in-person. Allow 3–5 business days for reproduction.

Contact your liaison or provider at PFC directly. Be sure to bring your picture ID, medical records, and relevant imaging related to your injury when you visit.

For immediate support, contact the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (call or text 988), available 24/7. You can also reach out to PFC's Behavioral Health team for guidance during clinic hours.
